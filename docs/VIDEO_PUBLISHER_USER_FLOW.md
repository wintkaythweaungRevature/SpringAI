# Video Publisher — User Flow (what you do as a user)

## Solo vs team (why roles exist — or don’t)

- **Most users (solo):** You subscribe for yourself. You upload, edit, and publish your own videos. You don’t need to “submit for review” or “approve” — you’re one person doing everything. So **you don’t choose a role.** The flow is: upload → review & edit → publish.
- **Teams (optional):** Some customers have several people: one creates content, another approves it, another hits “publish.” Then **roles** (Creator / Manager / Buffer) make sense. If your product is only for individuals, ignore roles and use the simple flow below.

**Bottom line:** If each user is posting for themselves, it’s one person: connect accounts → upload → edit drafts → publish. No role picker, no submit/approve.

---

## Before you start

- You must be **logged in** on the site.
- You need a **Member** subscription to use Video Publisher.
- You **connect your social accounts** (YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, etc.) once so the site can post for you.

---

## 1. Connect your social accounts (one-time)

1. Open **Video Publisher** from the sidebar.
2. In **“Connect your accounts”**, you see: YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, Facebook, X.
3. Click **Connect** next to each platform you want to use.
4. A popup opens → you sign in with that platform (e.g. Google for YouTube) and allow the site to post.
5. When you come back, that platform shows **Connected**. You can click **Refresh** if it doesn’t update.

**Result:** The site can now post to the accounts you connected. You only do this once per platform (unless you disconnect later).

---

## 2. Upload your video

1. Choose your **role** for this run:
   - **Creator** — you’ll upload, edit, and send for review.
   - **Manager** — you’ll approve or reject what others prepared.
   - **Buffer** — you’ll publish (or schedule) what’s approved.
2. Pick **which platforms** you want this video on (e.g. YouTube, Instagram, TikTok).
3. **Upload** your video file (drag & drop or click to choose). Supported: e.g. MP4, MOV, AVI.
4. Click **“Generate Content”**.

**What happens for you:**  
The site uploads your video and prepares one version per platform (captions, hashtags, format tips). You see a short “processing” step, then you’re taken to the next screen.

---

## 3. Review what the site prepared (AI drafts)

1. You see a **list of platforms** on the left (YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, etc.).
2. Click a platform to see **its draft**: caption, hashtags, and a short note about format (e.g. “60s Reel, vertical”).
3. You can **edit** the caption and hashtags for any platform.
4. Each draft has a **status**: e.g. Draft, Pending review, Approved, Rejected.

**What you’re doing:**  
Making sure the text and hashtags look right for each place before anyone approves or publishes.

---

## 4. Creator: Send for review

1. If you’re the **Creator**, when you’re happy with the drafts you click **“Submit for review”**.
2. The status of those posts changes to something like **“Pending review”**.

**What you’re doing:**  
You’re saying “I’m done editing; a manager can now approve or reject these.”

---

## 5. Manager: Approve or reject

1. If you’re the **Manager**, you see the list of platforms and their drafts.
2. For each one you can:
   - Click **Approve** → that post is allowed to be published.
   - Click **Reject** → that post won’t be published (you can leave a reason if the product supports it).

**What you’re doing:**  
You decide which of the Creator’s drafts actually go out to each platform.

---

## 6. Buffer: Publish (or schedule)

1. If you’re the **Buffer** (or the person who publishes), you see only the **approved** posts.
2. You click **“Publish approved (now)”** (or similar).
3. The site posts to each connected platform (YouTube, Instagram, etc.) using the caption and hashtags you approved.
4. When it’s done, you’re taken to the **Analytics** screen.

**What you’re doing:**  
You trigger the actual posting to social. Optionally, in the future you might also pick a **date and time** to schedule instead of “now”.

---

## 7. Viral trends and news

1. In **Video Publisher** you see a section **"Viral trends and news"** (or "What's trending").
2. It shows **lately viral trends** (e.g. hashtags, formats, topics that are blowing up on YouTube, Instagram, TikTok).
3. It shows **short news** or updates (e.g. platform algorithm changes, trending topics in your niche) so you can align your next video.
4. Use this to decide **what** to post and **when** — e.g. post when a trend is hot, or avoid a crowded topic.

**What you're doing:**  
Getting a quick snapshot of what's working right now so you can plan your next posts.

---

## 6. See how it did (analytics)

1. You see **which platforms** the video was published to (e.g. “✓ Live” next to YouTube, Instagram).
2. You see **numbers**: views, likes, comments, shares, engagement (per platform or total, depending on the product).
3. You see **AI insights**, for example:
   - “Your short clips get 4x more views than long ones.”
   - “Best time to post: Tuesday 7 PM.”
   - “Trending topic: #AITools — create content now.”
4. You see **next content ideas** the AI suggests (e.g. “5 AI tools that replace your team”).
5. If you want to do another video, you click **“+ New Video”** and start again from **Upload your video**.

**What you’re doing:**  
Checking performance and getting ideas for the next video, then optionally starting a new upload.

---

## End-to-end flow (as a user — solo)

```
Log in (Member) → Connect social accounts (once)
    → Upload video → Pick platforms → Generate Content
    → Review & edit AI drafts (caption, hashtags)
    → Publish now or Schedule (per platform: e.g. YouTube tomorrow 4 AM, Instagram 6 PM)
    → See analytics + insights + next ideas
    → Check Viral trends and news for what to post next
    → (+ New Video to repeat)
```

So: **connect accounts → upload → review & edit → publish or schedule (each platform can have its own time) → check analytics and viral trends.** You can also use **Viral trends and news** anytime to see what’s hot and plan your next video.
